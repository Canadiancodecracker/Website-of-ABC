SECTION 4: First-Aid Measures

Eye Contact: Rinse cautiously with water for at least 15 minutes. Remove contact lenses if present and easy to do. Continue rinsing. Seek immediate medical attention.

Skin Contact: Wash skin with plenty of soap and water for at least 15 minutes. Remove contaminated clothing. Seek medical attention if irritation persists.

Inhalation: Move to fresh air immediately. If breathing is difficult, give oxygen. Seek medical attention.

Ingestion: Rinse mouth with water. Do NOT induce vomiting unless directed by medical personnel. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. Seek immediate medical attention.

Notes to Physician: Treat symptomatically. Monitor thyroid function. May cause methemoglobinemia. This material is a suspected carcinogen and reproductive toxin.

SECTION 5: Fire-Fighting Measures

Extinguishing Media: Water spray, dry chemical, carbon dioxide (CO₂), alcohol-resistant foam

Unsuitable Extinguishing Media: None known

Special Hazards: May emit toxic fumes (sulfur oxides, nitrogen oxides, hydrogen sulfide) when heated to decomposition. Combustible material.

Protective Equipment: Wear self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) and full protective gear.

SECTION 6: Accidental Release Measures

  • Evacuate personnel to safe areas. Ensure adequate ventilation.
  • Wear suitable protective equipment (gloves, goggles, respirator, protective clothing).
  • Avoid dust formation. Do not breathe dust.
  • Prevent further leakage or spillage if safe to do so.
  • Do not let product enter drains or waterways.
  • Sweep up or vacuum up spillage and collect in suitable container for disposal.
  • Clean contaminated area thoroughly.
  • Dispose of contaminated materials as hazardous waste.

SECTION 7: Handling and Storage

Handling: Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing. Avoid formation of dust and aerosols. Use only in well-ventilated areas. Provide appropriate exhaust ventilation at places where dust is formed. Do not eat, drink, or smoke when using this product. Wash hands thoroughly after handling. Handle in accordance with good industrial hygiene and safety practice.

Storage: Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated place. Keep container tightly closed and sealed until ready for use. Store locked up. Store away from incompatible materials (oxidizing agents, acids). Protect from light and moisture.

SECTION 8: Exposure Controls / Personal Protection

Exposure Limits:

  • OSHA PEL: Not established
  • ACGIH TLV: Not established
  • NIOSH REL: Potential occupational carcinogen - minimize exposure

Engineering Controls: Use mechanical exhaust or laboratory fume hood to avoid exposure. Ensure eyewash stations and safety showers are close to the workstation location. Use explosion-proof equipment.

PPE:

  • Eyes: Chemical safety goggles and face shield
  • Skin: Wear impervious protective gloves (nitrile, neoprene) and protective clothing
  • Respiratory: Use NIOSH/MSHA approved respirator with HEPA filter if dust is generated or exposure limits are exceeded. In case of insufficient ventilation, use supplied-air respirator.
  • Body: Wear long-sleeved shirt, long pants, and lab coat. Avoid skin contact.

SECTION 10: Stability and Reactivity

Stability: Stable under recommended storage conditions. Decomposes on heating.

Conditions to Avoid: Heat, light, moisture, incompatible materials

Incompatibilities: Strong oxidizing agents, strong acids, heavy metal salts, peroxides, nitric acid

Hazardous Decomposition Products: Sulfur oxides (SOₓ), nitrogen oxides (NOₓ), hydrogen sulfide (H₂S), carbon oxides (CO, CO₂), ammonia

Hazardous Polymerization: Will not occur.

SECTION 11: Toxicological Information

Acute Toxicity:

  • LD50 Oral (Rat): 125 mg/kg
  • LD50 Dermal (Rabbit): > 2000 mg/kg
  • Skin Contact: May cause irritation
  • Eye Contact: Causes irritation
  • Inhalation: May cause respiratory irritation

Chronic Effects:

  • May cause thyroid damage with prolonged or repeated exposure
  • May cause bone marrow depression
  • May cause anemia

Carcinogenicity:

  • IARC: Group 3 (Not classifiable as to carcinogenicity to humans)
  • NTP: Reasonably anticipated to be a human carcinogen
  • OSHA: Not listed
  • GHS: Category 2 (Suspected human carcinogen)

Mutagenicity: Positive in some in vitro tests.

Reproductive Toxicity: Suspected of damaging fertility or the unborn child (Category 2).

SECTION 12: Ecological Information

Ecotoxicity:

  • LC50 Fish (96h): 200-500 mg/L
  • EC50 Daphnia (48h): 100-200 mg/L
  • Harmful to aquatic life with long-lasting effects

Persistence/Degradability: Biodegradable under aerobic conditions.

Bioaccumulation: Low potential for bioaccumulation.

Mobility in Soil: Expected to be mobile in soil when dissolved in water.
