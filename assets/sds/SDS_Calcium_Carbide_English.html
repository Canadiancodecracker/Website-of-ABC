SECTION 4: First-Aid Measures

Eye Contact: Rinse cautiously with water for at least 15 minutes. Remove contact lenses if present. Seek immediate medical attention.

Skin Contact: Brush off loose particles from skin. Immerse in cool water/wrap in wet bandages. Seek immediate medical attention. Do not use water if skin is dry and chemical is still present (brush off first).

Inhalation: Move to fresh air. If not breathing, give artificial respiration. Seek medical attention.

Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting. Rinse mouth. Drink 1 or 2 glasses of water. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. Call a physician immediately.

Notes to Physician: Treat symptomatically. Material is corrosive.

SECTION 5: Fire-Fighting Measures

Extinguishing Media: Dry chemical, dry sand, CO₂. DO NOT USE WATER.

Special Hazards: Contact with water releases flammable acetylene gas. May re-ignite after fire is extinguished.

Protective Equipment: Wear self-contained breathing apparatus and full protective gear.

SECTION 6: Accidental Release Measures

  • Wear suitable protective equipment (gloves, goggles, respirator).
  • Eliminate all ignition sources.
  • Do not touch damaged containers or spilled material unless wearing appropriate protective clothing.
  • Cover with dry earth, dry sand, or other non-combustible material followed by plastic sheet to minimize spreading or contact with rain.
  • Do not get water inside containers.

SECTION 7: Handling and Storage

Handling: Handle under inert gas. Protect from moisture. Do not breathe dust. Avoid contact with skin and eyes. Keep away from open flames, hot surfaces, and sources of ignition.

Storage: Store in a dry, cool, and well-ventilated place. Keep container tightly closed. Store away from incompatible materials and water.

SECTION 8: Exposure Controls / Personal Protection

Exposure Limits: Not established for Calcium Carbide. (Acetylene: Asphyxiant)

Engineering Controls: Use explosion-proof ventilation equipment. Ensure eyewash stations and safety showers are close to the workstation location.

PPE:

  • Eyes: Face shield and safety glasses
  • Skin: Wear appropriate protective gloves and clothing to prevent skin exposure
  • Respiratory: Follow the OSHA respirator regulations found in 29 CFR 1910.134 or European Standard EN 149. Use a NIOSH/MSHA or European Standard EN 149 approved respirator if exposure limits are exceeded or if irritation or other symptoms are experienced.

SECTION 10: Stability and Reactivity

Stability: Stable under recommended storage conditions. Reacts with water.

Incompatibilities: Water, moisture, oxidizing agents, acids.

Decomposition Products: Acetylene, Calcium Hydroxide.

Hazardous Polymerization: Will not occur.

SECTION 11: Toxicological Information

Acute Toxicity: Causes severe burns.

Effects: Inhalation may cause irritation to the respiratory tract. Ingestion causes severe swelling, severe damage to the delicate tissue and danger of perforation.

Carcinogenicity: Not listed by ACGIH, IARC, NTP, or OSHA.

SECTION 12: Ecological Information

Ecotoxicity: Reacts with water to form Calcium Hydroxide which increases pH of water, potentially harmful to aquatic life.

Persistence/Degradability: Reacts with water.

SECTION 13: Disposal Considerations

  • Dispose of in accordance with local regulations.
  • Do not flush to sewer.
  • Material reacts with water; disposal requires special handling.
