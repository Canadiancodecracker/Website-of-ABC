SECTION 4: First-Aid Measures

Eye Contact: Rinse cautiously with water for at least 15 minutes. Remove contact lenses if present and easy to do. Continue rinsing. Seek immediate medical attention.

Skin Contact: Remove contaminated clothing immediately. Wash skin with plenty of soap and water for at least 15 minutes. Seek medical attention if irritation persists.

Inhalation: Move to fresh air immediately. If breathing is difficult, give oxygen. If not breathing, give artificial respiration. Seek immediate medical attention.

Ingestion: Do NOT induce vomiting. Rinse mouth with water. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. Seek immediate medical attention.

Notes to Physician: Treat symptomatically. May cause methemoglobinemia. Monitor for respiratory distress.

SECTION 5: Fire-Fighting Measures

Extinguishing Media: Dry sand, dry chemical powder, carbon dioxide (CO₂). DO NOT USE WATER.

Unsuitable Extinguishing Media: Water, water spray, foam

Special Hazards: Contact with water or moisture releases flammable acetylene and ammonia gases. May re-ignite after fire is extinguished. Toxic fumes (nitrogen oxides, cyanamide) may be released during combustion.

Protective Equipment: Wear self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) and full protective gear. Fight fire from maximum distance or use unmanned hose holders or monitor nozzles.

SECTION 6: Accidental Release Measures

  • Evacuate personnel to safe areas. Eliminate all ignition sources.
  • Wear suitable protective equipment (chemical-resistant gloves, goggles, respirator, protective clothing).
  • Avoid dust formation. Do not breathe dust.
  • Do not allow contact with water or moisture.
  • Cover spill with dry earth, dry sand, or other non-combustible absorbent material.
  • Carefully collect material and place in appropriate container for disposal.
  • Do not get water inside containers.
  • Ventilate area after cleanup.

SECTION 7: Handling and Storage

Handling: Handle under inert gas or in a dry atmosphere. Protect from moisture and water. Avoid dust formation. Do not breathe dust. Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing. Use only in well-ventilated areas. Keep away from heat, sparks, open flames, and hot surfaces.

Storage: Store in a dry, cool, and well-ventilated place. Keep container tightly closed and sealed until ready for use. Store away from incompatible materials (water, acids, oxidizers). Protect from moisture and humidity. Use moisture-proof packaging.

SECTION 8: Exposure Controls / Personal Protection

Exposure Limits: Not established for Calcium Cyanamide.

Engineering Controls: Use explosion-proof ventilation equipment. Ensure eyewash stations and safety showers are close to the workstation location. Use local exhaust ventilation to control dust.

PPE:

  • Eyes: Chemical safety goggles and face shield
  • Skin: Wear chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile, neoprene) and protective clothing to prevent skin exposure
  • Respiratory: Use NIOSH/MSHA approved respirator with dust/mist filter (N95 or higher) if exposure limits are exceeded or if dust is generated. Use supplied-air respirator in confined spaces.
  • Body: Wear long-sleeved shirt, long pants, and chemical-resistant apron

SECTION 10: Stability and Reactivity

Stability: Stable under dry conditions. Unstable in the presence of moisture or water.

Conditions to Avoid:

  • Moisture, high humidity environments
  • Strong acids, strong oxidizers
  • Heat, sparks, open flames

Incompatibilities: Water, moisture, acids, oxidizing agents, alcohols

Hazardous Decomposition Products:

  • Ammonia (NH₃)
  • Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ)
  • Cyanamide
  • Acetylene (C₂H₂) - when in contact with water

Hazardous Polymerization: Will not occur.

SECTION 11: Toxicological Information

Acute Toxicity:

  • LD50 Oral (Rat): 1600–2000 mg/kg
  • Skin Contact: Causes irritation
  • Eye Contact: Causes serious irritation
  • Inhalation: Respiratory tract irritation

Chronic Effects: Repeated exposure may cause skin dryness and dermatitis.

Carcinogenicity: Not listed by ACGIH, IARC, NTP, or OSHA.

Mutagenicity: No data available.

Reproductive Toxicity: No data available.

SECTION 12: Ecological Information

Ecotoxicity: Harmful to aquatic life with long-lasting effects. Releases ammonia when wet, which is harmful to aquatic organisms.

Persistence/Degradability: Not readily biodegradable.

Bioaccumulation: Low potential for bioaccumulation.

Mobility in Soil: May be mobile in soil when dissolved in water.

SECTION 13: Disposal Considerations

  • Dispose of in accordance with local, regional, and national regulations.
  • Do not discharge directly into natural water bodies.
  • Do not flush to sewer or waterways.
  • Material must be neutralized before disposal.
  • Container must be properly labeled and sealed.
  • Consult with licensed waste disposal company.
